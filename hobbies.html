The Journey at a Glance

Click a milestone to read the story behind it.

2008 — First Stick on the Ice

I decided to play hockey because of the movie "Miracle on Ice." It was right around the time I was diagnosed with dyslexia, which made hockey feel like an escape.

2016 — Move to San Jose for Hockey

Growing up in Vancouver, Washington, there wasn't much hockey opportunity, so I got the chance to go play for a team down in San Jose, California. I was 14 at the time, and I decided to move away from my family to pursue that opportunity.

2018 — Boarding School in Connecticut

San Jose didn't feel like home, so I took another opportunity: going to play hockey at a boarding school called Hotchkiss. That meant moving across the country at age 16.

2019 — Career-Ending Injury

I ended up tearing a labrum in my hip that would have required surgery and stopped me from continuing in hockey. So I picked something else — and ended up falling in love with rowing.

2023 — LMU and the Transfer Portal

I got recruited to row at Loyola Marymount University, where I spent the next three years until the school cut the rowing program — forcing me to find a new home. That new home ended up being Northeastern. Those years at LMU were such a blessing, and I wouldn't have wanted them to go any other way.

2025 — Captain Year

Since coming to Northeastern I have had a bigger impact than I ever thought I would. I was named captain and received a couple of awards, as well as being nominated for the Herbert Gallagher Award, which recognizes outstanding leadership, dedication, and overall contribution to Northeastern athletics.

2026 — Currently

Right now I'm getting ready for our national championships over the next couple of days, finishing this site, and graduating from Northeastern.

2028 — LA

The biggest goal in my life is to make the Olympic team.

People should not be measured by their accomplishments but rather by how far they have come from where they started.
